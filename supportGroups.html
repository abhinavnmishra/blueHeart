Support Groups


When we are hurting, we often turn to our families and friends for support. But they may not always say the right words or do the right things. A support group gives you the space to share your experiences with a particular concern with people who share the same concern as you do.

Even though your experiences with it may differ, it can still be a space where you feel heard and seen. It may also help you understand your concern better.

Virtual Support Group For Parents Of LGBTQIA+ Community

We, as parents, may not understand what it means to be LGBTQIA+, or may not have the right things to say, ask or do.

However, with time, understanding, connection and acceptance, almost everything can improve.

TherapHeal invites parents of the LGBTQIA+ community to a support group dedicated to your experiences, challenges and achievements of coming out as parents of LGBTQIA+ folk. This support group is aimed at creating a safe, judgment-free space for people to share their lived experience and extend support to each other.

Virtual Support Group LGBTQIA+ Series

Queer children experience childhood differently compared to heterosexual folks due to a variety of reasons including lack of role models, supportive systems, and events like coming out and transitioning. This group aims to provide support for queer folks as we explore how queer timelines are different when compared with non-queer timelines.

The session would aim to explore their experiences and create a non-judgmental reflective space around this subject.
